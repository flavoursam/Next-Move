# Challenger Strategy

Use when: the prospect has a clear problem but may not understand it fully, the deal size justifies a bold approach, and confidence is medium or higher.

The Challenger play is to reframe how the prospect thinks about their situation — not to pitch, but to teach something they didn't know and then connect it to a specific next step.

## Core principles

**Teach → Tailor → Take Control**
- Teach: offer a specific insight or data point they haven't considered
- Tailor: connect it directly to their business situation (their software, their market, their season)
- Take Control: propose a clear next step — not "just checking in", not "let me know your thoughts"

**Commercial tension over comfort**
Do not default to a passive follow-up. If the deal is passive or slow, create a moment of decision. A well-placed challenge is more useful than a soft nudge.

**Specificity is credibility**
Vague claims destroy trust. Every insight must connect to something real — their TripAdvisor rating, their current software, their market position, their last conversation. If you cannot be specific, do not make the claim.

## Angle guidance

Lead with an insight that reframes the problem — not just "you have this pain" but "here's something about your situation that you might not have considered."

The angle should create mild tension: the prospect should feel slightly uncomfortable sitting still after reading or hearing it.

## Action guidance

- Prefer a reply-driving email or a live call over passive outreach
- Always propose a concrete next step: a specific meeting, a question to answer, a date to confirm
- Avoid "checking in", "touching base", or "following up" — these signal low value
- If the deal is stale, say why it's worth re-engaging now — not generically, but with a specific reason

## Draft tone

Confident, direct, short. Write like someone who has earned the right to give advice. Not arrogant — informed.

Example tone: "You're #1 on TripAdvisor in your region. That traffic should be converting to direct bookings at a higher rate than what Rezdy typically delivers internationally. Worth a 20-minute conversation to show you what that looks like?"

## Disqualification rule

If there is no real insight to offer and no credible reason to act now, do not force a Challenger angle. Flag the lead as low priority and recommend parking it or disqualifying.
