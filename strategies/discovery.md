# Discovery Strategy

Use when: CRM data is thin, confidence is low, this is a first meaningful contact, or we do not yet have enough information to make a credible claim.

The Discovery play is not a pitch. It is a genuine attempt to understand the prospect's situation before offering anything. Done well, a great discovery call does more pipeline work than three follow-up emails.

## Core principles

**Ask before you claim**
Do not make strong claims about their pain if you do not have evidence. You will lose credibility. Instead, ask a question that opens up the conversation and reveals whether there is something worth pursuing.

**Curiosity, not qualification**
The goal of discovery is to understand — not to tick boxes on a qualification checklist. Ask open questions that let the prospect talk about their business, their problems, and their priorities.

**One question is enough to open a door**
Do not send a list of questions. One well-chosen question creates more conversation than five generic ones.

## Angle guidance

The angle for discovery is not a pain point — it is a question or observation that signals you have done your homework and are genuinely curious about their situation.

A good discovery angle: "I noticed you're running Rezdy but I couldn't find a direct booking widget on your site — is that a deliberate choice or something you've been meaning to look at?"

A bad discovery angle: "Are you looking to improve your bookings?" — this is generic and signals low research.

## Action guidance

- Prefer a short, direct email with one honest question over a cold call on thin data
- The call to action should be a reply or a 15-minute conversation — not a full demo
- If the contact situation is unclear, a LinkedIn message to identify the right person is valid
- Do not offer a pitch or a product demo at this stage — earn the right to it first

## Draft tone

Curious, low-pressure, brief. Write like someone who noticed something interesting and wants to understand it better — not like someone running a qualification script.

Example tone: "I noticed you're ranked in the top 10 on TripAdvisor for your region but I couldn't find a way to book directly from your site. Curious whether that's intentional or something worth a quick chat about."

## Disqualification rule

If discovery reveals no real pain or no fit, recommend disqualification early. A fast no is better than a slow maybe. Flag this in rep_notes.
